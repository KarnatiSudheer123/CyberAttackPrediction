Proposed:
=========
The base paper provides a comprehensive analysis of how Artificial Intelligence (AI) techniques—spanning Machine Learning (ML), Deep Learning (DL), Natural Language Processing (NLP), Explainable AI (XAI), and Generative AI (GenAI)—can be applied to enhance cybersecurity. It primarily focuses on a conceptual and comparative study of these AI methods, evaluating their potential to address various cybersecurity challenges such as anomaly detection, threat prediction, and intelligence generation. The paper emphasizes improving transparency through XAI and exploring emerging trends in Generative AI for simulating and mitigating cyber threats. Overall, the base paper serves as a theoretical framework, offering valuable insights into the future direction of AI-driven cybersecurity research and applications.


Our Extension:
==============
Building upon the foundation established by the base paper, our project transforms these conceptual ideas into a fully functional, implementation-based study using the CICIDS2017 dataset. We developed and evaluated multiple AI models, including Machine Learning (DT, RF, LR, Naive Bayes, Voting Classifier), Deep Learning (DNN, CNN, LSTM, CNN+LSTM, CNN+LSTM+GRU), and Generative AI (VAE, GAN, DistilGPT2) for cyber threat detection. To enhance interpretability, XAI techniques (LIME and SHAP) were applied to visualize feature importance and model reasoning. Furthermore, we integrated the trained models into a Flask-based web application that allows real-time user interaction and prediction. This extension advances the base paper by demonstrating a complete end-to-end AI-driven cybersecurity system—covering data preprocessing, model building, explainability, and deployment—bridging the gap between theoretical research and practical implementation.
